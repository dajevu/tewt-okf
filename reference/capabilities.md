# Capabilities Map

## Purpose

This reference maps common customer situations to the Atteniv capability that best addresses each one. Use it to generate "how Atteniv can help" callouts in guides, proposals, and customer-facing materials. Each entry pairs a recognizable situation with a single primary capability so that callouts stay focused and actionable.

## How to use this map

1. Identify the customer situation described in a conversation, discovery note, or support ticket.
2. Locate the closest match in the table below.
3. Reference the linked capability in a callout using phrasing such as: *"Atteniv can help with this through **[Capability]**."*

When a situation spans multiple capabilities, lead with the primary one and mention secondary capabilities only if the customer asks for detail.

## Situation categories

For faster lookup, situations are grouped into four categories. Use these when browsing rather than searching for a specific phrase.

### Visibility

Customers who cannot see or understand their current state of access and identity.

- "We can't tell who has access to what." → [Access Visibility](./access-visibility.md)
- "We have no idea what changed after our last audit." → [Change Tracking](./change-tracking.md)
- "We're not sure our policies match what we actually enforce." → [Policy Drift Detection](./policy-drift-detection.md)

### Lifecycle

Customers struggling with the day-to-day flow of identities joining, changing, and leaving.

- "It takes weeks to onboard a new hire." → [Onboarding Automation](./onboarding-automation.md)
- "We keep finding dormant accounts." → [Lifecycle Hygiene](./lifecycle-hygiene.md)
- "Temporary access never gets revoked." → [Time-Bound Access](./time-bound-access.md)
- "Our contractors keep slipping through the cracks." → [External Identity Management](./external-identity-management.md)
- "Our service accounts are unmanaged." → [Service Account Governance](./service-account-governance.md)

### Governance

Customers who need structure, policy, and repeatable oversight.

- "Auditors want evidence we're reviewing access regularly." → [Access Review Automation](./access-review-automation.md)
- "We need to prove segregation of duties is in place." → [SoD Enforcement](./sod-enforcement.md)
- "People request access they don't need." → [Policy-Based Recommendations](./policy-based-recommendations.md)

### Experience

Customers where the process works technically but creates friction for the people involved.

- "Managers don't know what they're approving." → [Contextual Approvals](./contextual-approvals.md)

## Callout phrasing examples

The following examples show how to translate a situation-capability pairing into customer-facing language. Adapt the tone to the surrounding document.

> **How Atteniv can help:** Access Visibility gives you a clear, current picture of who can reach what across your environment—so you stop relying on guesses and spreadsheets.

> **How Atteniv can help:** Access Review Automation schedules recurring reviews, routes them to the right owners, and produces the evidence auditors expect—without a last-minute scramble.

> **How Atteniv can help:** Time-Bound Access lets you grant elevated or temporary permissions that expire on their own, so nothing lingers past its intended window.

## Notes on mapping

- **Lead with one capability.** Callouts land best when they point to a single, named capability. If a second capability is genuinely relevant, mention it briefly rather than listing alternatives.
- **Match the customer's words, not the product's vocabulary.** Keep the situation description recognizable; reserve capability naming for the recommendation.
- **When no situation matches**, default to [Access Visibility](./access-visibility.md) as a discovery starting point, since most Atteniv engagements benefit from establishing a baseline first.
